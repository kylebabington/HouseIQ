## CUSTOMER COMPLAINT

Customer reported that the air-conditioning system was running continuously but was not cooling the home below 78 degrees. Customer also reported reduced airflow from several upstairs vents and an unusual buzzing sound from the outdoor condenser unit.

---

## DIAGNOSTIC FINDINGS

Technician inspected the thermostat, indoor air handler, evaporator coil, electrical components, refrigerant pressures, outdoor condenser, and accessible ductwork.

The following conditions were found:

1. The dual-run capacitor in the outdoor condenser tested below the manufacturer’s acceptable range.

2. The condenser fan motor was slow to start due to the weakened capacitor.

3. The return-air filter was heavily restricted with dust and debris.

4. Refrigerant pressure was slightly below the expected operating range.

5. No visible refrigerant leaks were found during the initial electronic leak inspection.

6. The evaporator coil showed light surface dirt but was not frozen at the time of inspection.

7. Electrical connections were checked and tightened where necessary.

---

## REPAIRS PERFORMED

* Removed and replaced the failed 45/5 microfarad dual-run capacitor.

* Installed a new 16 x 25 x 1 pleated air filter.

* Added 1.0 pound of R-410A refrigerant to bring system pressures within the recommended operating range.

* Cleaned loose debris from the outdoor condenser cabinet.

* Tightened accessible electrical terminals.

* Tested condenser fan motor startup and operation.

* Verified thermostat operation.

* Measured supply-air temperature after repairs.

* Confirmed that the system was cooling properly before leaving the property.

## TECHNICIAN RECOMMENDATIONS

2. Schedule annual air-conditioning maintenance before the beginning of each cooling season.

3. Monitor system performance over the next several weeks.

4. If cooling performance decreases again, schedule a full refrigerant leak search.

5. Consider having the evaporator and condenser coils professionally cleaned during the next maintenance appointment.

6. Due to the age of the equipment, begin planning financially for possible system replacement within the next several years.
